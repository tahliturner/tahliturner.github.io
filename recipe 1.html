Alice in WonderlanD Cookies 

Ingredients

Sugar Cookies

  • 227g butter (room temperature)
  • 3 cups plain flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Checkboard Cookies

  • 2 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp water

Royal Icing

  • 2 large egg white (room temperature)
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 3 cups sifted icing sugar
  • Food colouring
  • Milk

Method

  1. In an electric mixer, beat together the butter and sugar till light and fluffy.
  2. Add in the eggs and vanilla extract and beat until combined.
  3. Then add in the flour, baking soda and salt mixing till it creates a dough.
  4. Take all the dough out of the mixing bowl execpt about 1/2 a cup and put it aside.
  5. With the left over dough that will be used for the checkboard cookies add 2 heaped tsp of cocoa powder and mix until combined.
  6. Wrap both doughs in cling wrap separetly, and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  7. Once chilled, preheat your oven to 180 degrees celsius.
  8. Bring out the dough, and take off another 1/2 cup of light-coloured dough for the checkerboard cookies and set aside.
  9. With the remainder dough, roll it out to about 1 cm thick and cut out the shapes.
  10. Tansfer to a baking tray and bake for 8-10 minutes or until golden brown.
  11. To make the checkboard cookies, roll out both the light-coloured and chocolate dough till they are about 1 inch thick.
  12. Cut into long strips around 1 inch wide.
  13. Whisk together the egg and water to create an egg wash.
  14. Wipe down the edges of the dough with the egg wash, and alternate the chocolate and light-coloured dough strips, pressing them together so they stick. On the bottom layer, alternate light, chocolate, light. On the next layer- chocolate, light, chocolate and on the top layer-light, chocolate, light.
  15. Tansfer to a baking tray and bake for 8-10 minutes or until golden brown.
  16. Once the cookies have finished baking set them on a cooing rack and allow to cool.
  17. To make the royal icing, combine the egg whites and lemon juice mixing till light and airy.
  18. Bit by bit add the icing sugar untill all combined.
  19. Divide the icing into seperate bowls and add the colours you want.
  20. Put the different coloured icing into seperate icing bags and snip a tiny bit off the end.
  21. Use this to pipe the icing around the edges of the cookies and allow to dry.
  22. Add a tsp of milk to the colouredicing and mix it in to make the icing runnier.
  23. Using the piping bag, fill the cookie to the edges with icing and let dry.
  24. Use the thicker icing to add the extra details on top and allow to dry.
